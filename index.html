Most of the value comes from OSW's own pages

Almost 87% of the visits OSW delivered came as referral traffic from its own web properties, the member listings, the blog, and Signature Experience pages, working quietly across the whole nine months.

The remaining 13% came from tagged outbound marketing: the Destination Ontario co-op ad drives and the OSW newsletter. That traffic skews top-of-funnel, which points to an awareness role rather than a closing one.

Overall findings

What the program returned

Between September 2025 and May 2026, Ontario's Southwest sent its members roughly 4,950 tracked website visits and an estimated $12,400 in booking value. The signal is strongest for the two operators with complete, end-to-end tracking, Homegrown Hideaway and Udderly Ridiculous, which together account for 4,450 of those visits and 53 of the 66 measured bookings. Two more operators show partial data, and three could not be measured this period. The headline is consistent across all of them: OSW's steady, always-on referral traffic is the engine, and it is converting.

Key findings

What the data tells us about OSW's marketing

Nine months of traffic and booking data from Ontario's Southwest member operators.

The headline

87% of tracked visits came through OSW's website

87% of all tracked visits came through OSW's own web properties: the blog, member listings, and Signature Experience pages. Advertising brought people to these pages, and the pages sent them to member sites. The website is where visitors become bookings.

What's working

Blog + listings = 3,700 visits

The OSW blog and direct listing referrals accounted for 75% of all traffic to member sites. Much of the blog traffic came from paid ads. Ads brought visitors to the blog, and the blog sent them to members.

Best conversion rate

Signature Experience pages: small but effective

The Signature Experience pages delivered only ~100 visits but had the highest booking rate of any channel. People who land on these pages are already planning a trip. More of these pages would mean more bookings.

Measured value

$12,400 in bookings

OSW-originated traffic produced 66 confirmed bookings worth an estimated $12,400. Homegrown Hideaway and Udderly Ridiculous had full tracking and account for most of this total.

How it works

Ads bring traffic to OSW; OSW sends it to members

Paid promotion drove visitors to OSW's blog. The blog sent those visitors to member sites. Advertising and content work together: ad spend reaches new audiences, and OSW's website routes them to members who can book them.

Data gap

We're missing part of the picture

Two members lost visit data to a 90-day retention limit. Three more couldn't be measured at all. The real value OSW delivers is higher than what's captured here. Better tracking would show the full return.

Bottom line

OSW's marketing works. Paid and owned channels support each other.

Advertising brings visitors to OSW's website, and the website sends them to members. The blog, listings, and Signature Experience pages turned ad spend into 66 bookings worth $12,400 across the members we could measure. To show the full return, fix the tracking gaps. To get more bookings, expand Signature Experiences.

Methodology & limitations

How these numbers were produced

Stated plainly so the figures can be trusted, and so the gaps point to where tracking should improve next.

01Sample & sources

Window: . Traffic was pulled from each member's Google Analytics 4 property, counting only sessions OSW originated, identified two ways:

  • On-website referral — sessions tagged from ontariossouthwest.com (source osw) plus untagged referral from that domain.
  • Off-website marketing — sessions from OSW's tagged outbound campaigns (the Destination Ontario co-op drives and the newsletter).

02How value is estimated

Purchase revenue is not captured in analytics, so value is modelled: measured bookings × each operator's average booking value. Those average values were supplied by each operator.

Bookings for Udderly, Long Point, and Cottonwood come from each operator's third-party booking software, and Homegrown's are tracked as on-site booking actions. All four are confirmed bookings, not intent.
