Session 01 • In-person • ~60 min Completed

AI Foundations & Your Workflow Audit

What these tools actually do well, where they fall short, and where they can save Twin Lions the most time.

Session summary

We started by getting honest about what these tools really are. Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini and Copilot aren't magic. Think of them as very fast drafters that are good at spotting patterns. They can get a first version of writing, a summary, or a reformat done in seconds, and they're only ever as good as the instructions you hand them.

We talked through the two ways teams get burned: trusting AI blindly, when it can be confidently wrong, and barely using it, which leaves easy time on the table. The whole point here is to get the thinking and drafting done faster, so the team spends its hours where they count.

Then we got to the heart of it. We went around the room and mapped the tasks that quietly eat the week. That audit became our menu for the next three sessions.

Key lessons

  • These tools are good drafters, but they don't really know things, so treat the first version as a starting point you read and sharpen before it goes anywhere.
  • What you put in decides what you get out. Give it a vague ask and you'll get a vague result; give it specifics and you'll get something you can use.
  • The tools don't know your business, so you have to hand over the context every time. A Claude Project fixes that by remembering it for you.
  • The repeatable jobs with a consistent shape are where AI earns its keep: scopes, notes, letters, postings. The one-off judgment calls still need a person.
  • The Copilot headaches come down to a filing and naming problem more than an AI one. The fix is to clean up the naming first and add the AI layer second.

One honest snag came up too. Copilot struggles to query a lot of the team's files. The root cause sits upstream: inconsistent invoice naming and filing means that finding one invoice can mean clicking through every scanned doc in a project folder, and scanned or merged PDFs make it hard to pull real costs by product, trade or supplier. The answer is to clean up the filing and naming first, then add an AI lookup layer on top. We'll treat that as its own project.

The #1 bottleneck we're building

Voted by the team

Client-facing scope descriptions

Turning internal budget line items into clear, plain-English scope the client can actually follow. Right now it's slow and tedious, and it comes up on every job. Because it repeats so often and follows a predictable shape, it's a smart place to start.

We proved it live. We took rough budget lines, had Claude turn them into client-ready scope, then refined the tone on the spot. We also started a Twin Lions Claude Project, loaded with the files we use for planning, scheduling and scoping, so the tools stop making us re-explain the business every time.
